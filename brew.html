How to Brew Vietnamese Coffee

Master the art of authentic Vietnamese coffee at home.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Filter

Place the pour-over filter over your cup or use a traditional phin filter for authenticity.

Step 2: Add Coffee Grounds

Use 2 tablespoons of Copper Cow Coffee for bold, balanced flavor.

Step 3: Bloom the Grounds

Pour just enough hot water (195–205°F) to saturate grounds. Let bloom for 30 seconds.

Step 4: Complete the Pour

Slowly add hot water in circles until the cup is full, allowing it to drip through naturally.

Step 5: Customize & Enjoy

Add sweetened condensed milk, ice, or a splash of creamer—make it yours!

Tools You’ll Need to Brew Vietnamese Coffee

  • Copper Cow Coffee Pour-Over Kit: An all-in-one kit for easy, mess-free brewing.
  • Phin Filter (Optional): Prefer tradition? Use a classic Vietnamese metal filter.
  • Glass or Mug: Your favorite cup to enjoy the experience.
  • Hot Water: Ideally between 195°F and 205°F for rich extraction.
  • Sweetened Condensed Milk (Optional): Add for the classic cà phê sữa đá taste.
  • Ice (Optional): Chill it down for that refreshing iced version.

A Brief History of Vietnamese Coffee

Introduced by French colonists in the 19th century, coffee quickly became part of Vietnam’s cultural fabric.

The phin filter — a small metal drip tool — brews bold, flavorful coffee slowly over sweetened condensed milk, creating the iconic cà phê sữa đá.

Over the years, Vietnam’s love for coffee led to innovations like egg coffee, coconut coffee, and more — each one steeped in creativity and heart.

Pro Tips

  • Use water just off the boil (195-205°F).
  • Let the coffee bloom for 30 seconds before pouring fully.
  • Add sweetened condensed milk for authentic cà phê sữa đá.
